## 🏃 Exercise Therapy — Updated 2026 Evidence

### 🗞️ Parkinson's Foundation + ACSM Joint Recommendations (February 17, 2026)
The **Parkinson's Foundation** and the **American College of Sports Medicine (ACSM)** released updated exercise recommendations for people with PD:

- Revised guidance to make exercise **more accessible and sustainable**
- Emphasis on **early intervention** and proactive physical function management
- Evidence-based resources to support active lifestyle maintenance

> 🔗 [Parkinson's Foundation — Updated Exercise Recommendations](https://www.parkinson.org/about-us/news/updated-exercise-recommendations)  
> 🔗 [ACSM — PD Exercise Recommendations Updated 2026](https://www.acsm.org/parkinsons-disease-exercise-recommendations-updated-2026/)

### 📚 Key Evidence Points
- PD is now the **world's fastest-growing neurological disorder** with rapidly rising prevalence
- Exercise is increasingly recognized as a **disease-modifying therapy** for PD (Medscape, 2026)
- Neurologists Indu Subramanian and Bas Bloem co-authored evidence supporting exercise as both **primary and secondary treatment**
- **Non-motor symptoms** (sleep, mood, cognition) also respond significantly to structured exercise
- A 2025–2026 evidence review in *Wiley/NP* confirmed exercise alleviates neuropsychiatric symptoms in PD

> 🔗 [PMC — Exercise for People with Parkinson's Disease: Updates and Future](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11382789/)

## ⚡ Quick Tips

1. **Exercise is medicine** — 150+ min/week of moderate activity is now considered disease-modifying, not just symptomatic management. Prioritize consistency over intensity.
2. **Find a trial** — [Fox Trial Finder](https://www.michaeljfox.org/trial-finder) connects patients directly to recruiting studies. Eligibility criteria are searchable.
3. **Watch alpha-synuclein therapies** — Multiple candidates in the pipeline targeting protein aggregation. The Anavex and FIU compounds are the most recent entrants (2026).
4. **Non-motor symptoms matter** — Depression, sleep disturbance, and constipation often precede motor symptoms. Exercise helps across the full spectrum.
5. **Stay connected** — The Parkinson's Foundation and local chapters offer free resources, support groups, and exercise programs specifically designed for PD.
